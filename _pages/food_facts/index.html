What exactly is Buckwheat - Is it safe for people who cannot metabolize gluten?

Contrary to belief, Buckwheat is in fact a fruit seed that is related to rhubarb and sorrel, not a cereal grain. This seed's coat or hull is a green or tan color, which makes the flour when grinded, a dark brown or black color hence its name "blé noir" (black wheat) or "sarrasin". It is a nutritive powerhouse, Low GI, Gluten Free and easy to digest.

Buckwheat can be safely eaten by people who have celiac disease as it does not contain gluten.

Its name is derived from the Dutch word brockweit, which means "beech wheat", reflecting buckwheat's beechnut-like shape and its wheat-like characteristics such as its size to wheat kernels and its possibilities of recipes as alternatives to using wheat products, while it does not contain any gluten at all.
